::equipment-story
By emitting ultra-short laser pulses, this spectrometer excites target atoms and captures emission spectrum footprints, measuring structural state dynamics at sub-picosecond timescales. 

This spectrometer is designed to capture atomic excitation responses under extremely short timescales, enabling research in quantum dots, light-emitting diodes, and organic photochemistry. Operators can tune the coherent pulse widths and capture photoluminescence decays with high spatial resolution. Highly stable optical benches prevent alignment shifts during intensive thermal cycles.
::

### Pre-run checklist
1. Verify optical alignments at low power before turning on key lasers.
2. Ensure cooling chillers are operational.
